The path ends at a low wall of stone and thorn. The keeper names the custom plainly: one measure of myrrh, laid in the stone dish by the entrance, before he will speak of the trees or their resin. A porter bars the stair to the college rooms. The brother inside receives captains only by introduction, and your standing with the Portuguese is not yet enough to carry you through the door.

The keeper will name specimens brought from the island and show the marks by which the better sort are known. He asks coin, not favor: 50 gold for each lesson. Mastery is offered only for goods gathered or prepared at this site.

The keeper rubs resin dust from his thumb and asks what cargo has brought you inland. His Portuguese is serviceable, but he waits for plain questions.

You ask how the red resin is gathered.

"Not from the trunk like frankincense. The tree bleeds where the bark is cut, and the best pieces harden clean. If it is dull, heavy with dirt, or smells of smoke, leave it."

He turns the question over.
